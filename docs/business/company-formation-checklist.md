# Company Formation Checklist

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-04
> **Status:** Pre-registration — action items for setting up the business entity

## Business Structure Decision

**Recommendation: Register as a Pty Ltd company.**

Universities generally won't sign a service agreement with a sole trader for
anything beyond a one-off invoice. A Pty Ltd signals legitimacy, provides limited
liability, and is expected by university procurement teams. The admin overhead is
manageable with modern accounting software.

| Structure | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sole trader | Simple, cheap, no ASIC fees | No liability protection, harder to contract with universities, less professional | Not recommended |
| Pty Ltd | Limited liability, professional credibility, easier to contract | ASIC fees ($310/year), annual review, more complex tax | **Recommended** |
| Trust + Pty Ltd | Asset protection, tax flexibility | Unnecessary complexity at this stage; adds $1K+ in setup | Revisit if revenue exceeds $200K |

## Pre-Registration (Do These First)

- [ ] **Choose a company name.** Check availability on ASIC's company name search
  (https://connectonline.asic.gov.au/). "Project Zeus" is a working title — decide
  whether this is the trading name or if the company will have a different
  registered name.
- [ ] **Decide on directors and shareholders.** For a single-founder company: you
  are the sole director and sole shareholder. A Pty Ltd requires at least one
  director who is an Australian resident.
- [ ] **Choose a registered office address.** This is a public record. Can be your
  home address, an accountant's address, or a virtual office. Consider a virtual
  office ($20–$50/month) if you don't want your home address on ASIC records.
- [ ] **Choose a principal place of business.** Can be the same as the registered
  office.

## Company Registration

- [ ] **Register the Pty Ltd with ASIC.** Cost: $538 (standard, 2-3 business days)
  or $578 (same-day). Register online at https://connectonline.asic.gov.au/ or
  through a registered agent. This generates your ACN (Australian Company Number).
- [ ] **Apply for an ABN.** Free, done through https://www.abr.gov.au/. You'll
  need the ACN from the step above. The ABN is issued immediately in most cases.
- [ ] **Register for GST.** Mandatory once turnover exceeds $75K/year, but register
  from day one to avoid retrospective compliance. Done as part of the ABN
  application or separately via the ATO Business Portal. GST registration allows
  you to issue tax invoices and claim GST credits on business expenses.
- [ ] **Register a business name (if different from company name).** If trading as
  "Project Zeus" but the company is registered as something else, register the
  business name with ASIC ($39 for 1 year, $92 for 3 years).

## Financial Setup

- [ ] **Open a business bank account.** Major banks (CBA, ANZ, NAB, Westpac) and
  neobanks (Up, Judo) all offer business accounts. You need the ACN and ABN first.
  Separate the business account from personal finances from day one.
- [ ] **Set up accounting software.** Xero is the standard for Australian small
  businesses (~$30/month). Connect to the business bank account for automatic
  transaction imports. Xero handles invoicing, BAS preparation, and financial
  reporting.
- [ ] **Find an accountant.** You need one for: initial company tax setup, quarterly
  BAS lodgement (if GST registered), annual company tax return, and general tax
  advice (e.g., whether to pay a salary or take dividends). Budget $2,000–$4,000/year.
  Look for someone who works with small tech/consulting businesses.
- [ ] **Set up invoicing.** Use Xero or a similar tool. University invoices must
  include: ABN, GST amount, your bank details (BSB + account number), and a payment
  reference. Some universities pay via purchase order — be ready to reference a PO
  number on invoices.

## Insurance

- [ ] **Professional indemnity insurance.** Covers claims arising from your
  professional advice (e.g., a university claims your recommendations led to wasted
  marketing spend). Most universities require $1M–$2M coverage as a condition of
  contracting. Providers: BizCover, CGU, Vero, QBE. Budget $1,200–$2,400/year
  depending on coverage level. Get quotes for $2M — the premium difference from
  $1M is often small.
- [ ] **Public liability insurance (optional).** Covers third-party injury or
  property damage. Less relevant for a data/analytics business, but some university
  procurement teams include it as a standard requirement. Usually bundled with
  professional indemnity. Budget: additional $300–$600/year.
- [ ] **Cyber liability insurance (optional).** Covers data breaches and cyber
  incidents. Lower priority since Project Zeus uses only public data and doesn't
  handle student PII. Consider adding it if you later store client login credentials
  or any confidential university data.

## Legal

- [ ] **Service agreement.** Engage a commercial lawyer to draft a master service
  agreement based on the [terms outline](service-agreement-outline.md). Budget
  $2,000–$5,000. Get it done before your first pilot — don't sign a pilot on a
  handshake.
- [ ] **Privacy policy.** Even though you don't handle PII, you'll want a basic
  privacy policy for your website (if you have one) and to satisfy university
  procurement checklists. A lawyer can draft this alongside the service agreement
  for minimal extra cost.
- [ ] **Terms of use (for dashboard access).** Simple terms governing how the client
  can use the dashboard: no redistribution, no scraping, access revoked on
  termination. Can be a short document or a click-through on the dashboard URL.

## Online Presence

- [ ] **Domain name.** Register a domain for the business (e.g.,
  projectzeus.com.au or similar). Use it for professional email and eventually a
  landing page. Cost: $15–$30/year for a .com.au.
- [ ] **Professional email.** Set up email on the business domain (e.g.,
  hello@projectzeus.com.au). Google Workspace ($10/month) or Microsoft 365
  ($8/month). Don't send cold outreach from a gmail.com address — it signals
  "side project" to university marketing directors.
- [ ] **LinkedIn company page.** Create a company page on LinkedIn. This is where
  your content marketing lives (per the [sales strategy](sales-strategy.md)).
  Link your personal profile to it.
- [ ] **Landing page (optional for Phase 1).** A simple one-page site explaining
  what Project Zeus does, with a contact form. Not essential for Phase 1 (the brief
  is the sales tool), but useful for credibility when prospects Google you after
  receiving an email.

## Compliance and Ongoing Obligations

- [ ] **ASIC annual review.** $310/year. ASIC sends a review statement; you confirm
  company details are current and pay the fee. Due on the anniversary of
  registration.
- [ ] **BAS lodgement.** If GST registered: quarterly Business Activity Statements
  due 28 days after the end of each quarter (28 Oct, 28 Feb, 28 Apr, 28 Jul). Your
  accountant or Xero handles this.
- [ ] **Company tax return.** Due by 28 February each year (or 15 May if lodging
  through a tax agent). Company tax rate: 25% for base rate entities (aggregated
  turnover < $50M).
- [ ] **Personal tax return.** If paying yourself a salary, PAYG withholding
  applies. If taking dividends, they're included in your personal tax return with
  franking credits. Your accountant will advise on the most tax-efficient approach.
- [ ] **Superannuation.** If you pay yourself a salary of $450+/month, you must
  contribute super at the current rate (12% in 2026). Can be paid into your own
  super fund.

## Estimated Setup Costs

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| ASIC company registration | $538 |
| Business name registration (if needed) | $39–$92 |
| Accountant (initial setup + first year) | $2,000–$4,000 |
| Lawyer (service agreement) | $2,000–$5,000 |
| Professional indemnity insurance (year 1) | $1,200–$2,400 |
| Domain + email (year 1) | $150–$200 |
| Accounting software (year 1) | $360 |
| **Total** | **$6,300–$12,600** |

## Priority Order

Do these in sequence — each step depends on the one before:

1. Choose company name and structure → Register Pty Ltd → Get ACN
2. Apply for ABN + GST registration
3. Open business bank account
4. Set up Xero + find an accountant
5. Get professional indemnity insurance
6. Engage lawyer for service agreement
7. Register domain + set up professional email
8. Create LinkedIn company page

Steps 1-4 can be done in a single week. Steps 5-8 can run in parallel over the
following 2-3 weeks.
