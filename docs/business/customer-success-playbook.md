# Customer Success Playbook

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-04
> **Status:** Draft — designed for Phase 1 pilot engagements (1-5 clients)

## Principle

A $20K pilot with a university is not a software subscription — it's a relationship.
The university is buying confidence that their marketing spend is well-directed.
Every interaction should reinforce that confidence and build the case for renewal.

---

## Pilot Lifecycle (6 Months)

### Week 0: Deal Signed

**Actions:**

- Send a welcome email to the primary contact confirming the engagement, timeline,
  and what happens next
- Request their preferred meeting cadence (monthly check-ins are the default;
  some clients may prefer quarterly)
- Ask for the names and emails of anyone on their marketing team who should have
  dashboard access (expect 2-5 people)
- Send the first invoice (50% of pilot fee)

**Welcome email should include:**

- Dashboard URL and any access credentials (password-protected link)
- Brief guide on how to navigate the dashboards (1-page PDF or short video)
- Timeline of deliverables (brief in week 2, strategy session in week 3, quarterly
  update in month 3)
- Your direct contact details for questions

### Week 1-2: Dashboard Access + Initial Brief

**Actions:**

- Deploy the dashboard with the client's institution selected as the default view
- Generate and deliver the personalised marketing intelligence brief
- The brief should follow the [UOW example](example-brief-uow.md) format: brand
  gap, field prioritisation, audience profiles, geographic targeting, campaign
  recommendations

**Quality check before sending:**

- All data points are current (check IVI, QILT, and UAC release dates)
- Institution name is correct everywhere (no copy-paste errors from template)
- Campaign recommendations reference specific programs from their CRICOS listing
- Peer comparisons use relevant institutions (same state, similar size — not
  comparing a regional university to Melbourne or Sydney)

### Week 3: Strategy Session

**Format:** 60-minute video call (Zoom/Teams/Google Meet — whatever they use)

**Attendees:** Aim for the marketing director plus 1-2 team members who run
campaigns day-to-day. The more people who see the dashboards, the more embedded
the product becomes.

**Agenda:**

| Time | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0-10 min | Recap the brief's key findings — confirm they've read it, ask what resonated |
| 10-35 min | Live dashboard walkthrough — show their data, drill into specific fields they care about, explore the state demand map. Let them drive questions. |
| 35-50 min | Campaign implications — map findings to their upcoming campaign calendar. "When does your next campaign cycle start? Here's what the data says you should prioritise." |
| 50-60 min | Next steps — what data would be most useful? Any specific fields or audiences to deep-dive on? When is the next check-in? |

**After the session:**

- Send a follow-up email within 24 hours summarising key discussion points and
  any action items
- If they raised questions you couldn't answer live, research and respond within
  the week

### Month 2: First Check-In

**Format:** 30-minute video call or email check-in (match their preference)

**Purpose:** Ensure the dashboards are being used and the brief is informing
decisions. This is an early warning system — if they're not using the product,
the renewal conversation will fail.

**Questions to ask:**

- "Has the brief or dashboard informed any campaign decisions so far?"
- "Is there a specific analysis that would be useful for your next planning cycle?"
- "Has anyone else on the team been exploring the dashboards?"

**Watch for red flags:**

- They haven't logged into the dashboard since week 1
- Only the primary contact has used it (team hasn't adopted)
- They say it's "interesting" but can't point to a specific decision it influenced

**If red flags appear:** Offer a second walkthrough specifically for the campaign
team (not just the director). Propose a focused analysis on their #1 priority
field or upcoming campaign. The goal is to make the product indispensable before
month 5.

### Month 3: Quarterly Brief Update

**Actions:**

- Refresh the brief with any new data releases since the initial brief:
  - IVI publishes monthly — check for quarterly vacancy trends
  - QILT releases annual data — if it falls in this window, major update
  - UAC Early Bird data — if preference rounds have occurred, update demand signals
  - Google Trends — refresh brand awareness and trending topics data
- Highlight what's changed: "IT vacancies in NSW grew 8% since your initial brief —
  reinforcing IT as a priority field"
- Deliver the update and offer a 30-minute call to discuss changes

**Send the second invoice** (remaining 50% of pilot fee) alongside the quarterly
update — the timing anchors the invoice to a deliverable, making payment feel
earned.

### Month 4: Mid-Pilot Review

**Format:** 30-minute call

**Purpose:** Honest assessment of whether the pilot is delivering value. This
conversation sets up the renewal discussion.

**Questions:**

- "On a scale of 1-10, how useful has the intelligence been for your team?"
- "Has it influenced budget allocation or campaign targeting decisions?"
- "What would make it more valuable in the second half of the pilot?"
- "Is there anyone else in the university (e.g., faculty marketing, international
  office) who could benefit from this?"

**If scoring 7+:** The renewal conversation will go smoothly. Start seeding it:
"We'll have a renewal conversation in month 5 — happy to put together options."

**If scoring below 5:** Diagnose the problem. Is the data not granular enough? Are
they not using it? Is someone else already providing this? Adjust deliverables for
the remaining months if possible.

### Month 5: Renewal Conversation

**Format:** Dedicated 45-minute meeting

**Timing:** Start this conversation no later than 4 weeks before the pilot ends.
University budget cycles are slow — if they need to get approval, they need lead
time.

**Structure:**

1. **Review pilot value:** Summarise what was delivered, what decisions it informed,
   and any metrics of impact (even anecdotal: "You reallocated $X from Science to
   Health campaigns based on the opportunity gap data")
2. **Present renewal options:**

| Option | Price | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| Annual subscription | $30K–$50K | Continuous dashboard access, monthly data refreshes, quarterly briefs, 2 strategy sessions/year |
| Extended pilot (3 months) | $10K–$15K | If they need more time to build an internal case for annual budget |
| One-off brief refresh | $7.5K | If budget won't support annual but they want periodic updates |

3. **Ask for referrals:** "Is there a colleague at another university who might
   benefit from this?" A warm introduction from an existing client is worth 10
   cold emails.

**University budget context:** Most Australian universities set budgets in
October–November for the following calendar year. If your pilot ends mid-year,
the renewal may need to be funded from the current year's discretionary budget.
If it ends near the budget cycle, position the annual subscription as a line item
for next year's budget.

### Month 6: Pilot Wrap-Up

**Actions:**

- Deliver a final brief summarising the full pilot period and any emerging trends
- If renewed: transition to the annual service cadence (see below)
- If not renewed: send a professional wrap-up email, revoke dashboard access, and
  leave the door open ("If your situation changes, we'd love to work together
  again")
- Regardless of outcome: ask for a testimonial quote and permission to use them as
  a reference (even a one-sentence LinkedIn recommendation is valuable)

## Ongoing Service Cadence (Annual Subscribers)

| Frequency | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monthly | Data refresh — update dashboards with latest IVI, Google Trends data |
| Quarterly | Brief update — written analysis of changes and emerging trends |
| Bi-annually | Strategy session — 60-minute call aligned with university planning cycles |
| Annually | Full brief refresh — comprehensive update incorporating all new annual data (QILT, ABS, UAC) |
| Ad hoc | Email support for questions about the data or methodology |

invest in CRM software until you have more clients than you can track in your head.

## Handling Common Situations

### Client stops using the dashboard

Reach out directly: "I noticed the team hasn't been in the dashboards recently —
is there something we could improve or a specific analysis that would be more
useful?" Offer a focused walkthrough on their current campaign priority.

### Client asks for something outside scope

If it's a small request (a specific data cut, a chart for a presentation): do it.
The goodwill is worth more than the 30 minutes of work. If it's a large request
(a full additional analysis, a new data source): scope it as an add-on. "That's a
great idea — it's outside the pilot scope but I could do a deep-dive on [X] for
an additional $[amount]. Or we could include it in the annual subscription."

### Client wants to share dashboards with other departments

Encourage this — it expands the product's footprint within the institution and
makes renewal easier to justify. Offer to do a short walkthrough for the new
audience (e.g., faculty marketing team, international office).

### Primary contact leaves the university

This is the biggest risk to renewal. Act quickly: ask the departing contact for an
introduction to their replacement, and offer a fresh strategy session to bring the
new person up to speed. Also maintain a relationship with at least one other person
on the team throughout the pilot.

### Client pushes back on price at renewal

Anchor against the alternatives: a comparable consulting engagement would cost
$50K–$100K and produce a single point-in-time report. An in-house data analyst
to build and maintain this pipeline would cost $120K+ per year. Project Zeus is
the most cost-effective option for continuous marketing intelligence.
