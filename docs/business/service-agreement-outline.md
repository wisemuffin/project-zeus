# Service Agreement — Terms Outline

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-04
> **Status:** Outline for lawyer review — NOT a legal document
>
> This document outlines the key terms and clauses needed in a service agreement
> between Project Zeus (the supplier) and a university client. Take this to a
> commercial lawyer to draft the actual agreement. Budget $2,000–$5,000 for a
> template that can be reused across clients with minor customisation.

## Agreement Structure

The agreement should be structured as:

1. **Master service agreement** (general terms, reusable across all clients)
2. **Schedule / Statement of Work** (client-specific scope, pricing, timeline —
   changes per engagement without re-signing the master terms)

This structure means you draft the master agreement once, then attach a short
schedule for each new client.

---

## Key Clauses

### 1. Parties

- **Supplier:** [Project Zeus Pty Ltd / trading name] ABN [number]
- **Client:** [University name] ABN [number]
- Authorised representatives for both parties (who can sign, who receives notices)

### 2. Services

- Reference the attached Schedule for specific scope
- General description: "market research and analytics services using publicly
  available government data sources, delivered as interactive dashboards and written
  marketing intelligence briefs"
- Explicit statement: services are **advisory in nature** and do not constitute
  marketing execution, campaign management, or enrolment guarantees

### 3. Term and Renewal

- Initial term: as specified in the Schedule (e.g., 6 months for a pilot)
- Renewal: either auto-renewal with 30 days' notice to cancel, or manual renewal
  requiring a new Schedule
- **Recommendation for pilots:** Manual renewal (don't auto-renew a pilot — you
  want to have the renewal conversation explicitly)

### 4. Fees and Payment

- Fees as specified in the Schedule
- Payment terms: invoice on commencement, net 30 days (universities often require
  net 30; some are net 60)
- GST: all fees exclusive of GST; GST added to invoices if supplier is registered
- Late payment: interest at [RBA cash rate + 2%] on overdue amounts
- **Note for lawyer:** Some universities require the ability to pay by purchase
  order. Include a clause accepting POs as a valid ordering mechanism.

### 5. Data and Methodology

This is the most important section for Project Zeus specifically:

- **Data sources:** All data used in deliverables is sourced from publicly available
  government and research datasets. Specify the sources: Jobs and Skills Australia,
  QILT, UAC, VTAC, SATAC, ABS, CRICOS, Google Trends.
- **No client data required:** The supplier does not require access to, and will not
  request, the client's internal systems, student records, or personally
  identifiable information.
- **Data accuracy disclaimer:** Data is sourced from third-party government
  publications. The supplier does not warrant the accuracy, completeness, or
  timeliness of the underlying source data. The supplier warrants that it will
  accurately represent and attribute the source data in its deliverables.
- **Methodology transparency:** The supplier will provide attribution for all data
  points in deliverables, including source organisation, dataset name, and
  publication date.
- **No guarantee of outcomes:** Deliverables contain market intelligence and
  recommendations. The supplier does not guarantee any specific enrolment, marketing,
  or business outcomes from acting on the recommendations.

### 6. Intellectual Property

- **Supplier IP:** The underlying data models, analytics platform, crosswalk
  mappings, and methodology remain the supplier's intellectual property.
- **Client licence:** The client receives a non-exclusive, non-transferable licence
  to use the deliverables (dashboards, briefs, data exports) for their internal
  marketing and planning purposes during the term.
- **No sublicensing:** The client may not resell, redistribute, or sublicense the
  deliverables to third parties.
- **Client materials:** Any materials provided by the client (logos, branding
  guidelines) remain the client's IP.
- **Post-termination:** Upon termination, the client retains any briefs and data
  exports already delivered. Dashboard access is revoked. The client may continue
  to use previously delivered materials for internal purposes.

### 7. Confidentiality

- Both parties agree to keep confidential any non-public information shared during
  the engagement.
- **Carve-outs:** Information that is publicly available, independently developed,
  or required by law is not confidential.
- **Case studies:** The supplier may reference the client as a customer and describe
  the general nature of the engagement in marketing materials, unless the client
  opts out in writing.
- **Duration:** Confidentiality obligations survive for 2 years after termination.

### 8. Privacy

- The supplier does not collect, store, or process personal information as part of
  the services.
- If any personal information is inadvertently shared (e.g., in email
  correspondence), the supplier will handle it in accordance with the Australian
  Privacy Principles.
- **Note for lawyer:** Universities are subject to state privacy legislation (e.g.,
  PPIPA in NSW, PDPA in VIC). The agreement should acknowledge this without
  creating unnecessary obligations, given that the supplier doesn't handle student
  data.

### 9. Liability and Indemnity

- **Limitation of liability:** The supplier's total liability is limited to the
  fees paid under the relevant Schedule.
- **Exclusion of consequential loss:** Neither party is liable for indirect,
  consequential, or incidental damages (including lost revenue, lost enrolments,
  or lost data).
- **Indemnity:** Each party indemnifies the other against claims arising from their
  own breach of the agreement, negligence, or wilful misconduct.
- **Insurance:** The supplier will maintain professional indemnity insurance of at
  least $[1M–2M] for the duration of the engagement.
- **Note for lawyer:** Some universities have standard indemnity clauses that are
  heavily one-sided. Be prepared to negotiate — the key position is that liability
  should be capped at fees paid, and the supplier should not accept unlimited
  liability for a $20K pilot.

### 10. Termination

- Either party may terminate with 30 days' written notice.
- Immediate termination for material breach (with 14 days to cure, if the breach
  is curable).
- Upon termination: outstanding fees are payable for services delivered; dashboard
  access is revoked; the client retains previously delivered briefs and exports.
- **No refunds** for the portion of the term already elapsed. If terminated early
  by the client, fees are prorated based on months elapsed.

### 11. Dispute Resolution

- Good faith negotiation in the first instance (14 days).
- Mediation before litigation (through the Resolution Institute or equivalent).
- Governing law: [State/Territory] of Australia.
- Jurisdiction: Courts of [State/Territory].

### 12. General

- Entire agreement clause (supersedes prior discussions)
- Amendment only by written agreement signed by both parties
- No assignment without consent
- Severability
- Force majeure
- Notices (email is acceptable for operational notices; formal notices require
  written delivery)

## Schedule Template

The Schedule attached to the master agreement should contain:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Client name | University of [X] |
| Engagement type | Pilot — Marketing Intelligence |
| Term | 6 months from [start date] |
| Deliverables | Dashboard access, initial brief, quarterly update, strategy session, data exports |
| Fees | $20,000 ex GST |
| Payment schedule | 50% on commencement, 50% at month 3 |
| Authorised contacts | [Name, title, email] for each party |
| Special conditions | [Any client-specific terms, e.g., specific reporting requirements] |

## Notes for the Lawyer

1. **Keep it short.** Universities deal with massive contracts for IT systems and
   construction. A 5-page agreement for a $20K engagement signals low risk and
   makes procurement easier. If the master agreement exceeds 8 pages, it's too long.

2. **University procurement quirks:** Many universities require vendors to accept
   their standard purchase order terms. These may conflict with your agreement.
   Include a clause establishing that the master agreement prevails over PO terms
   in case of conflict.

3. **Insurance:** Check whether professional indemnity insurance at $1M is
   sufficient for university requirements. Some institutions require $2M or even
   $5M. Get quotes for $2M — the premium difference is often small.

4. **ABN and GST:** The supplier should be registered for GST before issuing
   invoices (mandatory once revenue exceeds $75K). Universities expect tax invoices
   with GST clearly stated.

5. **Electronic signatures:** Confirm that the university accepts electronic
   signatures (most do, but some legacy procurement teams still want wet signatures).
