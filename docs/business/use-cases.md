# How Project Zeus Drives University Marketing

Project Zeus combines 8 government and public data sources to answer the three questions every university marketing team faces: **what to promote, where to run campaigns, and who to target** — backed by employment data, graduate outcomes, and real search behaviour.

## Campaign Decisions

### 1. What should we promote?

**Field prioritization** — Not all fields deserve equal marketing spend. Zeus ranks every field of study into actionable tiers based on a composite of employer demand, graduate employment rates, and salary data.

| Tier | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **No-brainer** | High employer demand + strong employment + strong salary | Lead campaigns — these fields sell themselves with data |
| **High potential** | High employer demand, outcomes still developing | Lead with demand growth and career opportunity angles |
| **Proven outcomes** | Saturated student interest, but graduates do well | Lead with outcomes ("88% employment, $80K salary") rather than scarcity |
| **Challenging** | Low demand + weaker outcomes | Deprioritize or find niche angles (diversity, regional need) |

**Source dashboards:** [Field Value Proposition](/outcomes/field-value), [Opportunity Gap](/insights/opportunity-gap)

**Example:** *"Health has the largest opportunity gap in Australia — employer demand for Health graduates is 15 percentage points higher than student interest. Allocate budget accordingly."*

### 2. What should the ad say?

**Proof points for every field** — Generic "study with us" messaging loses to specific, data-backed claims. Zeus provides the numbers for each field of study:

- **Employment rate:** *"87% of Engineering graduates find full-time work within 4 months"*
- **Salary:** *"Health graduates earn a median $78K — 12% above the sector average"*
- **Demand growth:** *"IT job vacancies grew 23% in the last 12 months"*
- **Student satisfaction:** *"Rated above sector average for teaching quality and skills development"*

Each field also gets a composite **marketing signal** — a single label that tells the creative team which angle to lead with: career outcomes, demand growth, or both.

**Source dashboards:** [Graduate Outcomes](/outcomes/graduate-outcomes), [Student Satisfaction](/outcomes/student-satisfaction), [Emerging Occupations](/demand/emerging-occupations)

**Example:** *"Your Bachelor of Nursing ad should lead with: '92% full-time employment within 4 months, $72K median salary, and Health vacancies grew 18% this year.' That's three proof points sourced from QILT and Jobs and Skills Australia."*

### 3. Where should we run it?

**State-level targeting** — Graduate job demand varies dramatically by state. Zeus ranks every state by graduate vacancies per 1,000 youth, so you know where the labour market story is strongest.

- ACT has the highest graduate vacancy density — 3x the national average
- WA and NT have the fastest vacancy growth
- Engineering demand concentrates in WA; Health in TAS, SA, and NT; IT in ACT

**LGA-level targeting** — For digital campaigns (Google Ads, Meta), broader state targeting wastes budget. Zeus identifies the specific Local Government Areas with the highest youth concentration. In NSW, the top 20 LGAs by youth population cover over 50% of the state's 15-19 year olds — target those postcodes and save spend on low-density areas.

**Source dashboards:** [State Demand](/insights/state-demand), [State x FOS Demand](/demand/state-fos-demand), [Audience Density by LGA](/audiences/audience-density)

**Example:** *"Run your Engineering campaign in WA (highest state-level demand skew) targeting the Perth metro LGAs of Stirling, Joondalup, and Wanneroo — those three LGAs alone hold 25% of WA's youth population."*

### 4. Who should we target?

**Audience segmentation by field** — Different fields attract different people. Zeus profiles each field across three dimensions:

- **Gender split:** Engineering skews 70%+ male preferences — a female-targeted campaign has a clear underrepresentation angle. Health skews female — male-targeted messaging can broaden the pipeline.
- **Mature learner affinity:** Fields where Non-Year 12 applicants over-index (e.g. Health, Education) respond well to career-changer messaging and flexible study options.
- **Interstate draw:** Fields with high interstate preference ratios (e.g. Creative Arts) attract nationally — justify national campaign spend. Fields with low ratios are state-specific — keep budgets local.

**Segment-specific messaging** — For each applicant type (Year 12, mature age, interstate), Zeus shows which fields they over-index on, enabling segment-specific ad creative rather than one-size-fits-all campaigns.

**Source dashboards:** [Audience Profiles](/demand/audience-profiles), [Segment Playbooks](/audiences/segment-playbooks), [Gender Pay Gap](/outcomes/gender-pay-gap)

**Example:** *"Non-Year 12 applicants are 1.4x more likely to preference Health than the general pool. Run a career-changer campaign targeting 25-40 year olds in high-density LGAs with messaging: 'It's not too late — Health graduates earn $78K with 92% employment.'"*

## Strategic Planning

### 5. Is our brand working?

**Brand gap analysis** — Zeus compares every university's Google Trends search interest against its QILT quality ranking. A university that ranks #10 for quality but #30 for search interest has a clear brand awareness problem — and a clear case for marketing investment.

- **Interest-quality gap:** Positive = under-searched relative to quality (marketing opportunity). Negative = brand already exceeds quality position.
- **Geographic brand gaps:** Search interest by state reveals where a university is strong (brand stronghold) vs weak (untapped market).
- **Momentum tracking:** 3-month vs prior 3-month interest ratio shows which brands are accelerating, stable, or declining.

**Source dashboards:** [Brand Awareness](/audiences/brand-awareness), [Institution Scorecard](/audiences/institution-scorecard)

**Example:** *"University X ranks #8 nationally for graduate employment but #25 for search interest. Their strongest state is NSW but they're barely searched in QLD — a state with high graduate demand. There's a clear case for a QLD-focused brand campaign."*

### 6. Which programs should we invest in?

**Program-level recommendations** — Zeus connects ~9,000 CRICOS course listings to opportunity gap and graduate outcomes data. A university can filter to their own programs and see which courses sit in high-opportunity fields with strong outcomes — those are the programs to feature on landing pages, in prospectus materials, and in paid search.

**Source dashboards:** [Course Listings](/audiences/course-listings)

**Example:** *"Your university offers 12 courses in Health across 3 campuses. Health has the largest opportunity gap nationally, 92% FT employment, and $78K median salary. Feature these programs prominently in your course finder and run dedicated landing pages for each campus location."*

### 7. What's trending right now?

**Timely content hooks** — Zeus monitors Google Trends daily for trending searches in Australia and matches them to fields of study. When a Health-related news story spikes, a university can publish a social post or blog within hours linking the trend to their relevant programs.

**Source dashboards:** [Trending Interests](/demand/trending-interests)

**Example:** *"'Nursing shortage' is trending with 5,000+ searches today. Your Nursing program is in a field with a +15% opportunity gap. Post on social: 'Australia needs nurses — here's how to start your career in 3 years' with a link to your Bachelor of Nursing page."*

## The Pitch

We combine 8 government data sources to tell you exactly which programs to promote, what the ad should say, where to run it, and who to target — with proof points.

Instead of guessing which fields to feature in your next campaign, you get a ranked list backed by job vacancy data, graduate employment rates, and salary figures. Instead of running the same ads nationally, you get state-by-state and LGA-level targeting based on where youth populations and job demand actually concentrate.

Every recommendation traces back to a public, auditable data source — Jobs and Skills Australia, QILT, the ABS, and three state admissions centres covering 72% of Australian applicants.

## Data Sources

| Source | What it provides | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **UAC** (Universities Admissions Centre) | Student preferences by field, gender, applicant type | NSW/ACT (~40% of applicants) |
| **VTAC** (Victorian Tertiary Admissions Centre) | Student preferences by field and gender | VIC (~25% of applicants) |
| **SATAC** (SA Tertiary Admissions Centre) | Student preferences by field and gender | SA/NT (~7% of applicants) |
| **Jobs and Skills Australia** | Internet job vacancies by occupation, state, skill level | National, monthly |
| **QILT** | Graduate employment, salary, student satisfaction | National, 42 universities |
| **ABS** | Youth population by LGA and state | National, ~544 LGAs |
| **CRICOS** | Course listings by institution, field, location | National, ~9,000 courses |
| **Google Trends** | Search interest for universities + trending topics | National + state-level |
