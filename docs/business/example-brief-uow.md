# Example: University of Wollongong — Marketing Intelligence Brief

> This is an example of the marketing intelligence that Project Zeus produces for a university, using real data from 8 government and public sources.

## Brand Position: High quality, low awareness

UOW has a **significant brand awareness gap** — and that's actually the opportunity.

| Metric | UOW | Sector Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Quality rank** | **#4 out of 42** | Top 10% nationally for student satisfaction |
| **Search interest rank** | **#26 out of 42** | Bottom half for Google search interest |
| **FT employment rank** | #32 | Below sector average |
| **Salary rank** | #27 | Below sector average |
| **Awareness tier** | Medium | — |

UOW is the **4th highest-rated university in Australia for overall student experience** but ranks only 26th for how often people search for it. That's a 22-position gap between quality and awareness — one of the largest in the country. The marketing story writes itself: *"Australia's best-kept secret in higher education."*

## Where is UOW's brand strongest?

Surprisingly, **not in NSW**.

| State | Interest | Rank (for UOW) | Rank (in state) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ACT** | 7.7 | **1st** (strongest) | 18th in ACT |
| NT | 3.8 | 2nd | 32nd |
| SA | 3.2 | 3rd | 27th |
| **NSW** | 1.9 | **7th** (second weakest) | 30th in NSW |
| VIC | 0.6 | 8th (weakest) | 34th |

UOW's brand is strongest in Canberra (2.5 hours south) but gets drowned out in NSW by Sydney's universities. In NSW, UOW ranks **30th out of 42 universities** for search interest — behind institutions with far lower quality scores.

**Implication:** Double down on the ACT corridor where UOW already has brand momentum. In NSW, focus geographically on the Illawarra/South Coast and South-West Sydney rather than competing head-to-head with Sydney CBD institutions.

## Which programs to promote

UOW offers **522 course-location combinations** across 9 fields. Three fields sit in the top opportunity tier nationally:

| Field | Opportunity Gap | FT Employment | Median Salary | UOW Courses | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Health** | **+10.2%** (rank #1) | 87.2% | $78,583 | 77 (9 bachelor programs) | **Strong** |
| **IT** | **+7.2%** (rank #2) | 67.8% | $75,300 | 31 (7 bachelor programs) | **Demand** |
| **Management & Commerce** | **+4.4%** (rank #3) | 78.5% | $72,000 | 107 (9 bachelor programs) | **Strong** |
| **Education** | +0.1% (rank #4) | 86.8% | $78,800 | 35 (8 bachelor programs) | **Strong** |
| Engineering | -2.8% (rank #6) | 85.5% | $80,000 | 37 | Proven outcomes |

**Health is UOW's #1 marketing priority.** It has the largest opportunity gap nationally (+10.2%), strong outcomes (87% employment, $78K salary), vacancy growth of +7%, and UOW has deep course depth (Bachelor of Nursing alone is offered across multiple campuses). Health also has the highest mature learner index (1.3x) — career-changer messaging resonates here.

**IT is #2.** Huge demand gap (+7.2%) though employment outcomes are moderate (68%). The story is about career opportunity and demand growth rather than current outcomes. IT skews male and local (interstate draw 0.47) — keep campaigns NSW-focused.

**Engineering is a "proven outcomes" play.** The demand gap is slightly negative (saturated) but outcomes are the strongest of any field (85.5% employment, $80K salary). Lead with outcomes, not scarcity.

## Where to geo-target in NSW

Wollongong LGA has 13,748 youth aged 15-19 (rank #11 in NSW). But UOW's catchment extends into South-West Sydney — a massive youth corridor:

The **Sutherland–Campbelltown–Wollongong corridor** (Sutherland Shire through South-West Sydney down to Illawarra) represents UOW's natural geographic advantage over Sydney CBD universities — proximity, commutability, and lower competition for attention. These 5 LGAs alone hold 60,000+ youth.

NSW also over-indexes nationally on **IT demand** (specialisation rank #1 in Australia) — reinforcing IT as a key field for UOW's NSW campaigns.

## Campaign recommendations

### Campaign 1: Health career-changers (national)

- **Audience:** Women 25-40, career changers
- **Geography:** National (interstate draw ratio 1.59) with emphasis on ACT (brand stronghold)
- **Message:** *"Switch to a career that's actually hiring. 87% of Health graduates find full-time work within 4 months, earning $78K. Australia's #4 rated university for student experience."*
- **Programs to feature:** Bachelor of Nursing, Bachelor of Exercise Science, Bachelor of Public Health

### Campaign 2: IT local demand (NSW)

- **Audience:** Males 17-35, school-leavers and career changers
- **Geography:** NSW only (low interstate draw), targeting Wollongong + SW Sydney LGAs
- **Message:** *"IT has 7% more employer demand than student interest — and NSW leads Australia for IT jobs. Start your career at a university ranked #4 for student experience."*
- **Programs to feature:** Bachelor of Computer Science, Bachelor of IT

### Campaign 3: Brand awareness (ACT corridor)

- **Audience:** All prospective students 17-19
- **Geography:** ACT + South Coast NSW
- **Message:** *"Australia's #4 university for student satisfaction — just 2.5 hours from Canberra."* Lean into the quality-over-fame narrative.
- **Goal:** Convert existing brand awareness (UOW's #1 state for search interest) into applications

### Campaign 4: Illawarra/SW Sydney geo-play (local)

- **Audience:** Year 12 school-leavers
- **Geography:** Hyper-local — Sutherland, Campbelltown, Wollongong, Shellharbour postcodes
- **Message:** Field-specific proof points (Health, Engineering outcomes) + proximity advantage over Sydney CBD universities
- **Programs:** Full portfolio across all fields

## Data sources

All numbers in this brief are sourced from public government and research data:

| Source | What it provides |
|---|---|
| **QILT** (Quality Indicators for Learning and Teaching) | Student satisfaction rankings, graduate employment rates, median salaries |
| **Jobs and Skills Australia** (Internet Vacancy Index) | Job vacancy volumes by occupation, state, and skill level |
| **UAC** (Universities Admissions Centre) | Student preferences by field, gender, applicant type (NSW/ACT) |
| **VTAC** / **SATAC** | Student preferences for VIC and SA/NT |
| **ABS** (Australian Bureau of Statistics) | Youth population by LGA and state |
| **CRICOS** | Course listings by institution, field, and campus location |
| **Google Trends** | University search interest by state and over time |
