# Content Calendar — Phase 1

> **Last updated:** 2026-03-04
> **Status:** Draft — 3-month plan aligned with Phase 1 sales strategy

## Strategy

Content serves one purpose in Phase 1: **make your name familiar to university
marketing directors before your cold email arrives.** This is not brand building
for its own sake — it's warm-up for the outreach cadence in the
[sales strategy](sales-strategy.md).

### Channels

| Channel | Purpose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn (personal profile)** | Primary channel — domain expertise, data insights, sector commentary | 2-3 posts/week |
| **LinkedIn (company page)** | Reposts from personal + product updates once company is registered | 1 post/week |
| **Email newsletter (optional)** | Longer-form analysis for engaged contacts | Monthly (start in month 2 if there's demand) |

Personal profile posts outperform company page posts for reach and engagement.
Lead with the personal profile; use the company page for credibility.

### Content Pillars

Every post should map to one of these four pillars:

1. **Data insights** — Charts, findings, and surprising data points from the
   Project Zeus pipeline. These demonstrate the product without pitching it.
2. **Sector commentary** — Reactions to sector news (budget cuts, policy changes,
   enrolment trends). Positions you as someone who understands the market.
3. **Methodology / how we think** — Explain the data joins, the classification
   crosswalks, the logic behind opportunity gaps. Builds credibility with
   analytically-minded buyers.
4. **Campaign thinking** — "If I were running campaigns for [field], here's what
   the data says." Demonstrates practical value for marketing teams.

## Month 1: Establish Presence (Weeks 1-4)

Aligned with LinkedIn warm-up phase of the sales strategy.

### Week 1

| Day | Post | Pillar | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | **"We mapped the brand awareness gap for every Australian university."** Scatter plot of quality rank vs search interest rank (anonymised — don't name institutions). Tease: "The biggest gap is 22 positions between quality and awareness." | Data insight | High-engagement format — people want to know where their university sits. Don't name institutions yet — forces DMs. |
| Wed | **React to a sector news story.** E.g., international student cap updates, university budget cuts. Short take (3-4 sentences) with a data angle: "This is why data-driven domestic recruitment matters more than ever." | Sector commentary | Engage with comments from university marketers. |
| Fri | **"Health has the largest opportunity gap in Australian higher education."** Bar chart showing opportunity gap by field. Brief explainer of what opportunity gap means. | Data insight | Introduce the core concept without jargon. |

### Week 2

| Day | Post | Pillar | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | **"What does 'opportunity gap' actually mean?"** Short post explaining the methodology: employer demand (IVI vacancies) vs student interest (UAC preferences). The gap tells you where marketing can make a difference vs where students are already flooding in. | Methodology | Educational content — positions you as rigorous, not just opinionated. |
| Wed | **"If I were running Health campaigns for a mid-tier university..."** Walk through the targeting logic: Health skews female + high mature learner index → career-changer women 25-40. They can picture using this for their own campaigns. |
| Fri | **Share/comment on someone else's post.** Find a university marketing professional's post and add a substantive comment with a data point. | Engagement | Don't just post — participate in the community. |

## Month 3: Build Authority (Weeks 9-12)

Aligned with walkthrough and conversion phase. Content should reinforce expertise
for prospects in decision-making mode.

### Week 9-12 Post Ideas (schedule flexibly)

- **"What mid-tier universities can learn from how the Go8 recruit."** Comparative
  analysis without being adversarial.
- **"The regional university challenge: competing for students who can study anywhere."**
  Relevant to half the target list.
- **Case study format (anonymised):** "A university discovered their #1 opportunity
  field was being completely ignored in their campaign mix. Here's what the data
  showed." (Only publish if you have permission or the example is sufficiently
  anonymised.)
- **"What a marketing intelligence brief looks like."** Show the structure (not the
  full content) of a university brief. Screenshots of dashboard pages. Build
  curiosity.
- **Conference content:** If ATEM or EPHEA events are coming up, post about what
  you'd present / share. Positions you for future speaker slots.

## Engagement Rules

- **Respond to every comment** on your posts within 24 hours. Conversations in
  comments boost reach.
- **Comment on 3-5 other people's posts per week.** Prioritise university marketing
  professionals, ATEM members, and higher ed sector commentators.
- **Never pitch in posts or comments.** The brief is the sales tool. Content builds
  credibility and opens doors — the pitch happens in DMs and email.
- **Repost sparingly.** Original content outperforms reposts. Save reposts for
  genuinely relevant sector news.
- **Tag people only when genuinely relevant.** Don't tag university marketing
  directors in posts — it feels salesy. Let them find you through content quality
  and engagement.

## Measuring What Works

Track these monthly:

| Metric | Target (Month 1) | Target (Month 3) |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn connections (university marketers) | +20 | +50 |
| Average post impressions | 500 | 1,500 |
| DMs or comments from target prospects | 2-3 | 5-10 |
| Profile views per week | 50 | 150 |
| Inbound enquiries from content | 0 | 1-2 |

If posts consistently get under 200 impressions after month 1, experiment with
format (charts outperform text-only), posting times (Tue-Thu mornings tend to
perform best for B2B), and content pillar mix.
