## Phase 1 Execution Plan (12 Weeks)

### Weeks 1–2: Brief Generation Sprint

Generate personalised marketing intelligence briefs for all 10 target universities.
Use the [UOW example brief](example-brief-uow.md) as the template.

Each brief needs three elements that make it impossible to ignore:

1. **A surprising finding** about their institution (e.g., UOW's "ranked #4 for
   quality but #26 for awareness")
2. **One specific campaign recommendation** with audience, geography, message, and
   programs named
3. **A comparison to a peer** (e.g., "You rank below [peer] for search interest
   despite higher QILT scores")

The brief should take 10 minutes to read and leave them wanting more.

### Weeks 3–4: LinkedIn Warm-Up

Build visibility with target contacts before sending cold emails. The goal is that
when the email arrives, the sender's name is vaguely familiar.

- **Connect** with the marketing director / head of student recruitment at each
  target (personalised note, not a pitch)
- **Post 2–3 pieces of content** demonstrating domain expertise:
  - "We mapped every Australian university's brand awareness gap — here are the
    biggest surprises" (without naming specific institutions)
  - A chart showing the national opportunity gap by field of study
  - A post about the convergence of international caps and managed growth funding
- **Engage** with posts from university marketing professionals (comment
  thoughtfully, not generically)

### Weeks 5–6: Cold Outreach (First Touch)

Send the brief via email with a short, non-salesy cover note. The brief does the
selling — the email just needs to get them to open the PDF.

**Outreach cadence:**

| Day | Channel | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Email | Brief attached with cover note |
| Day 3 | LinkedIn | Message referencing the email |
| Day 8 | Email | Follow-up referencing one specific finding from their brief |
| Day 15 | Email | Final follow-up with a different angle (link to dashboard page or new data point) |

### Weeks 7–10: Walkthroughs and Conversion

For anyone who engages, book a 30-minute screen-share walkthrough using the live
Evidence dashboard.

**Walkthrough structure:**

| Time | Section |
|---|---|
| 5 min | "Here's what we do — we combine 8 government data sources into targeting intelligence" |
| 15 min | Walk through *their* data — brand position, top opportunity fields, audience profiles, geographic targeting. Let them ask questions and explore. |
| 5 min | "We're offering a 6-month pilot at [price] — dashboard access, quarterly brief updates, and a strategy session" |
| 5 min | Next steps — who else should see this? Can we send the brief to their team? |

### Weeks 10–12: Pilot Proposals

For warm prospects, send a one-page pilot proposal. Keep it simple — scope, price,
timeline. Price under $25K to stay below procurement thresholds.

## Objection Handling

### "We already have access to this data"

You're right — the data is all public. The value is in the joins. You'd need to map
ANZSCO occupation codes to UAC field categories, normalise QILT study areas, and
join ABS population data at LGA level. We maintain those crosswalks and update them
automatically. Your team could build this, but it would take months — and it's not
their core job.

### "We use a consulting firm for this"

Consulting gives you a point-in-time snapshot. We give you a continuously updated
picture. Our pipelines refresh from source data automatically, so when IVI publishes
new vacancy numbers or QILT releases new outcomes data, your dashboards update. No
repeat engagement fees.

### "Our budget is frozen / we're cutting costs"

That's exactly why this matters. You're not spending more on marketing — you're
spending the same budget smarter. If Health has a 10% opportunity gap and your
current campaigns over-index on Science, reallocating existing spend could improve
yield without adding a dollar.

### "What's the ROI?"

If reallocating even 10% of your campaign budget toward higher-opportunity fields
converts one additional student in a $30K course, the pilot pays for itself. And the
intelligence applies across your entire campaign portfolio — not just one field.

### "We need to go through procurement"

Understood. The pilot is designed to sit under your discretionary spending
threshold — $15–25K for 6 months. That typically doesn't trigger a formal tender.
Once we've proven value, we're happy to go through procurement for a full engagement.
